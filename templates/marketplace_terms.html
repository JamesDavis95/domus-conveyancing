Biodiversity Net Gain Marketplace Terms

1. Introduction

These Marketplace Terms govern your participation in the Domus Planning Biodiversity Net Gain Marketplace ("Marketplace"). These terms supplement our main Terms of Service and apply specifically to marketplace transactions.

2. Marketplace Overview

The Marketplace facilitates transactions between:

  • Landowners/Suppliers: Parties offering biodiversity net gain credits
  • Developers/Buyers: Parties seeking to purchase biodiversity credits
  • Domus Planning: Platform provider and transaction facilitator

3. Eligibility and Registration

3.1 General Requirements

  • Must be at least 18 years old or a legally constituted entity
  • Must provide accurate identification and business information
  • Must comply with all applicable environmental and planning regulations
  • Must have legal authority to enter into transactions

3.2 Landowner Requirements

  • Must provide proof of land ownership or legal right to manage the land
  • Must have completed or committed to completing qualifying habitat management
  • Must obtain necessary planning permissions and environmental assessments
  • Must maintain appropriate insurance coverage

3.3 Developer Requirements

  • Must have a genuine need for biodiversity net gain credits
  • Must comply with the Biodiversity Net Gain regulations
  • Must provide project details and offset requirements

4. Credit Creation and Verification

4.1 Credit Standards

All biodiversity credits must meet or exceed:

  • UK Biodiversity Net Gain regulations and statutory requirements
  • Natural England guidance and assessment methodologies
  • Local planning authority requirements
  • Recognised habitat management and creation standards

4.2 Verification Process

  • Independent ecological assessment and verification
  • Habitat condition assessment using approved methodologies
  • Management plan review and approval
  • Legal agreement documentation and registration

4.3 Monitoring and Maintenance

Credit suppliers must:

  • Maintain habitats for a minimum of 30 years
  • Provide annual monitoring reports
  • Allow access for verification inspections
  • Remedy any habitat deterioration or non-compliance

5. Transaction Process

5.1 Listing Credits

Landowners may list verified credits with:

  • Detailed habitat information and biodiversity unit calculations
  • Geographic location and accessibility details
  • Pricing and availability timelines
  • Management plan and monitoring commitments

5.2 Credit Purchase

Developers may purchase credits through:

  • Direct purchase of available credits
  • Request for proposal (RFP) process for specific requirements
  • Forward contracting for future credit delivery
  • Bulk purchasing for multiple projects

5.3 Contract Formation

Legally binding contracts are formed when:

  • Buyer accepts seller's offer or vice versa
  • Payment is processed successfully
  • Digital contract is executed by both parties
  • Credit transfer is recorded on the platform

6. Pricing and Fees

6.1 Credit Pricing

  • Sellers set their own prices per biodiversity unit
  • Prices may vary based on habitat type, location, and timing
  • Platform provides market data and pricing guidance
  • All prices are exclusive of VAT where applicable

6.2 Platform Fees

  • Transaction Fee: 7% of the total transaction value
  • Payment Processing: Stripe fees (typically 1.4% + 20p)
  • Verification Services: Separate fees may apply for optional services
  • Legal Documentation: Additional fees for complex contracts

6.3 Payment Terms

  • Payment is due at the time of credit purchase
  • Funds are held in escrow until credit delivery is confirmed
  • Sellers receive payment within 5 business days of delivery
  • All payments are processed through Stripe Connect

7. Quality Assurance and Warranties

7.1 Seller Warranties

Sellers warrant that:

  • They have legal title or authority over the land
  • Credits are genuine and meet stated specifications
  • All necessary permits and approvals have been obtained
  • There are no material adverse conditions affecting the land

7.2 Platform Role

Domus Planning:

  • Facilitates transactions but is not a party to credit agreements
  • Provides verification services and quality checks
  • Maintains transaction records and documentation
  • Does not guarantee the performance of credits or parties

7.3 Dispute Resolution

In case of disputes:

  • Parties are encouraged to resolve issues directly
  • Platform mediation services are available
  • Independent expert determination may be required
  • Legal remedies remain available to all parties

8. Compliance and Regulatory Requirements

8.1 Environmental Compliance

  • All activities must comply with environmental protection laws
  • Credits must meet Biodiversity Net Gain statutory requirements
  • Planning permissions must be obtained where required
  • Protected species and habitats must be properly considered

8.2 Financial Compliance

  • All transactions are subject to applicable tax obligations
  • Anti-money laundering regulations apply
  • Financial reporting requirements must be met
  • VAT registration may be required for high-volume sellers

9. Risk Management

9.1 Credit Risk

  • Credits may lose value due to regulatory changes
  • Habitat management may fail to achieve expected outcomes
  • Planning requirements may change over time
  • Market demand and pricing may fluctuate

9.2 Insurance Requirements

  • Sellers must maintain appropriate public liability insurance
  • Professional indemnity insurance is recommended
  • Environmental liability coverage may be required
  • Proof of insurance must be provided upon request

10. Data and Documentation

10.1 Record Keeping

  • All transactions are permanently recorded on the platform
  • Credit certificates and legal documentation are maintained
  • Monitoring data and reports are stored securely
  • Access to historical data is provided to relevant parties

10.2 Reporting Requirements

  • Annual monitoring reports must be submitted
  • Compliance certificates must be renewed as required
  • Any material changes must be reported promptly
  • Third-party audits may be required periodically

11. Termination and Suspension

11.1 Account Termination

We may terminate marketplace access for:

  • Violation of these marketplace terms
  • Failure to meet quality or compliance standards
  • Fraudulent or misleading activities
  • Non-payment of fees or charges

11.2 Effect of Termination

  • Existing contracts remain valid and enforceable
  • Outstanding obligations must still be fulfilled
  • Platform access and new transactions are suspended
  • Data and documentation remain accessible for compliance

12. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law:

  • Our liability is limited to the transaction fees paid to us
  • We are not liable for the performance or quality of credits
  • We do not guarantee regulatory compliance by users
  • Users are responsible for their own due diligence

13. Modification of Terms

We may modify these marketplace terms with 30 days' notice to active users. Continued use of the marketplace constitutes acceptance of modified terms.
